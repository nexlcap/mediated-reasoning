Problem: WebMCP is an emerging W3C browser standard (Chrome 146, Feb 2026) that lets websites expose typed, callable tools to in-browser AI agents via a new navigator.modelContext API — replacing fragile DOM scraping with structured function calls. Co-developed by Google and Microsoft, it is the client-side counterpart to Anthropic's Model Context Protocol. Should a software company invest in building WebMCP support into their web product now, and what does that decision entail across market positioning, technical implementation, cost, legal/privacy risk, and scalability?

Adaptive Module Selection:
  Selected modules: strategy, tech, cost, risk, market, standards_adoption, ai_ecosystem_integration
  Reasoning: This is a strategic technology investment decision requiring analysis of: (1) strategy - how WebMCP fits into business positioning and competitive advantage; (2) tech - implementation complexity and technical feasibility of the emerging standard; (3) cost - investment requirements and financial implications of early adoption; (4) risk - uncertainties around an emerging standard and timing of market adoption; (5) market - demand for AI-enabled web experiences and competitive landscape dynamics. These five perspectives cover the core business decision framework for evaluating early adoption of this emerging web standard.
  Ad-hoc modules: standards_adoption, ai_ecosystem_integration
  Gap check: The selected modules provide strong coverage of core business considerations, but there are two significant gaps for this emerging browser standard decision: (1) The timing and adoption dynamics of emerging web standards require specialized analysis beyond general market assessment - understanding browser rollout patterns, developer adoption curves, and standard maturation cycles is critical for investment timing. (2) This decision involves deep integration with AI agent ecosystems and emerging Web3/AI infrastructure that requires specialized technical-strategic analysis beyond traditional tech implementation concerns.
